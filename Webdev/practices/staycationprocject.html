Bear Lake-Utah

The Bear Lake

Bear Lake, nicknamed the "Caribbean of the Rockies" for its intense turquoise blue water, is one of the best locations to visit in Utah because of its stunning, shallow sandy beaches, vibrant summer boating, and unique year-round recreation. Located on the Utah-Idaho border, it offers swimming, fresh raspberry shakes, hiking, and fishing, making it ideal for families and outdoor enthusiasts.


reasons people visit Bear Lake:

  • Stunning Turquoise Water: The lake's unique turquoise color gives it a tropical appearance.
  • Family-Friendly Beaches: Beaches like North Beach and Rendezvous Beach feature shallow, warm, and sandy shores, making them perfect for children.
  • Water Recreation: The lake is popular for boating, sailing, jet skiing, water skiing, and paddleboarding.
  • Unique Local Culture & Food: The area is famous for its fresh raspberry shakes, especially during the annual Raspberry Days festival in Garden City.
  • Year-Round Activities: While summer is the peak season for water sports, winter offers activities like snowmobiling and ice fishing, including the unique Cisco Run in January.
